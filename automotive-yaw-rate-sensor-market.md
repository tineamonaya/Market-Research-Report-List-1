<p><ul><li>Piezoelectric Type</li><li>Micromechanical Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive yaw rate sensor market is primarily segmented into two types: piezoelectric and micromechanical. Piezoelectric sensors leverage the piezoelectric effect to detect angular velocity through changes in electrical charge in response to mechanical stress, offering high sensitivity and robust performance. In contrast, micromechanical sensors, often based on MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems) technology, utilize tiny mechanical structures to measure changes in motion. Both types are essential for vehicle stability control and navigation systems, contributing to advancements in automotive safety and performance.</p></p>
