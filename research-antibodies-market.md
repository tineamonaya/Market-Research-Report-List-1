<p><p>The research antibodies market primarily consists of monoclonal and polyclonal antibodies. Monoclonal antibodies are identical and derived from a single clone of B cells, offering high specificity and consistency in research applications. In contrast, polyclonal antibodies consist of a mixture of antibodies from different B cell lines, providing a broader range of target recognition but with greater variability. Both types serve crucial roles in areas such as diagnostics, drug development, and basic research, each with unique advantages tailored to specific research needs.</p></p>
