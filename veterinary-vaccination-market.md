<p><p>The veterinary vaccination market consists of several types, including live attenuated vaccines, inactivated vaccines, and others. Live attenuated vaccines contain weakened forms of pathogens, providing strong immune responses with long-lasting protection. Inactivated vaccines are made from killed pathogens, ensuring safety but often requiring multiple doses for effective immunity. Other market types may include subunit vaccines, DNA vaccines, and vector-based vaccines, each offering unique benefits for preventing diseases in various animal species, enhancing overall animal health and productivity.</p></p>
