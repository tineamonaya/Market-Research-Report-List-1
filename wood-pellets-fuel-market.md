<p><p>The wood pellets fuel market consists mainly of two types: white pellets and black pellets. White pellets are made from clean wood residues, offering high energy density and lower emissions, making them suitable for residential and industrial heating. Black pellets, produced from dense lignin-rich biomass, are designed for co-firing with coal in power plants. They have a higher moisture content and lower energy density compared to white pellets, but are advantageous for reducing carbon emissions and utilizing waste materials in energy production.</p></p>
