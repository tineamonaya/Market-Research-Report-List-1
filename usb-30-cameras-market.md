<p><p>The USB 3.0 cameras market primarily features two sensor types: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor). CCD cameras excel in low-light conditions, offering superior image quality and reduced noise, making them suitable for applications like scientific imaging and professional photography. In contrast, CMOS cameras are generally more cost-effective, consume less power, and provide faster data processing, which is ideal for high-speed imaging and consumer electronics. Each type serves distinct needs across various industries.</p></p>
