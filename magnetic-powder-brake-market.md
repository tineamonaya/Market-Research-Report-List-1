<p><p>The magnetic powder brake market consists of two primary types: coil rotary and coil stationary. Coil rotary brakes feature a rotating coil that creates a magnetic field to generate braking force, making them ideal for dynamic applications requiring precision control. In contrast, coil stationary brakes use a fixed coil to exert braking torque on a stationary element, suitable for applications where stability and control during halting are critical. Both types enhance operational efficiency through precise torque management and reliable performance in various industrial settings.</p></p>
