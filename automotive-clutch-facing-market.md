<p><ul><li>Dry Type Clutch Facing</li><li>Wet Type Clutch Facing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive clutch facing market comprises two primary types: dry type and wet type clutch facings. Dry type clutch facings operate without lubrication, relying on friction materials to engage and disengage the engine from the drivetrain, commonly used in manual transmissions. In contrast, wet type clutch facings function within a lubricated environment, utilizing fluid to enhance performance and cooling, typically found in automatic transmissions and some motorcycles. Each type offers distinct advantages in applications, durability, and performance characteristics.</p></p>
